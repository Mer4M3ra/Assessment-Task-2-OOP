## Individual Impact
My game trains players' rapid decision-making and strategic thinking as they choose which attribute to compare each round, developing data literacy and pattern recognition through a balance of skill and luck. However, it risks biasing users toward expensive or high-performance cars if price or power is ranked too highly, subtly teaching that a higher score equals a better car in real life. To counter this, I will allow players to set their own attribute weights, display rankings transparently, and use plain-language labels so non-experts can participate. As a designer, my responsibility is to ensure no single attribute dominates, present cars as having strengths and weaknesses rather than an overall winner.
## Social Impact
My game encourages communication and friendly competition as players announce attributes, compare values, and debate surprising outcomes, but uneven card distribution can create unfair advantages. To fix this, I will shuffle decks so each player receives a mix of high and low performers, and I will include a team mode to reduce individual pressure and promote collaboration. For inclusivity, I will use icons alongside numbers and avoid technical jargon, ensuring the game is accessible to all skill levels in a classroom setting. Clear data sources and a simple rulebook will prevent disputes, while the competitive format keeps players engaged and talking about cars in a constructive way.
## Environmental Impact
My digital game reduces paper waste compared to physical cards, though device electricity use has a small carbon footprint, making it more environmentally friendly than producing physical decks overall. By including fuel economy and CO₂ emissions as measurable attributes, I teach players that efficiency matters and that electric cars like the BYD Seal can be powerful in an eco-category. However, if these attributes are ranked low, the game effectively ignores sustainability, so I will give them moderate to high ranking and add a five-year fuel cost visual to make financial benefits tangible. To further encourage eco-thinking, I will include a carbon cost metric showing how many trees would offset the car's annual emissions, turning each round into a subtle lesson about environmental impact.
## Legal Impact
Using real car brand and model names like Lamborghini and BYD is permitted as nominative fair use, but I will avoid manufacturer logos and copyrighted images and instead use generic car icons or public domain silhouettes to prevent infringement. As a school project, I am protected by educational fair dealing provisions, but I still must cite my data source and include a disclaimer that values are approximate and for entertainment only. To avoid misleading users, I will show data sources and dates on each card, use value ranges where data is uncertain, never use the word recommend, and include a report error feature so users can flag inaccuracies. This fulfils my legal and ethical duty to present fair and honest information.
