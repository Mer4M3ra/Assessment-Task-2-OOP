## How the game works
### How a round is played
- Each player is dealt and equal amount of cards from the deck
- the current player chooses an attribute from their card
- all players revea their card
- the selected attribute is then compared across the players
### How attributes are selected
- the active player chooses one attribute per round
- the attribute must be one of the given options like price or fuel capacity
### How Winners Are Determined
- the player with the best value for the chosen attribute wins the round
- the winner collects all cards and adds to bottom of their deck
### What happens in a draw
- if two or more players have the same values the cards go into a draw pile and another round is played then the next winner collects all cards in the pile
### How the game ends
- The game continues until:

    One player has all the cards or all other players run out of cards
- that player is declared the winner

## Game Balance
### What is it?
Game balance refers to how fair and evenly matched the gameplay is so that no player has an unfair advantage. In a balanced game, success should depend on both strategy and chance, rather than one player consistently winning due to stronger cards or attributes.
### Potential Unfair advantage
An issue is that the outcome of the game can sometimes depend heavily on the initial card distribution. A player who receives stronger overall cards at the start may have an advantage that is not based on skill, but on luck.
To address these issues, the game includes multiple attributes with different value types so some values are better when its low and some are better when its high, which helps reduce the dominance of any single strategy. An improvement could be adding a rule where attributes cannot be repeatedly chosen in consecutive turns, or introducing random attribute selection to increase unpredictability and fairness.
